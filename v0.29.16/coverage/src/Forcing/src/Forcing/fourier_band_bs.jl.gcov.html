       9              : Forcing based on Biot–Savart energetics within a given range of wavenumbers.
      10              : 
      11              : This type of forcing does _not_ rely on an imposed normal fluid velocity. Instead, it starts from the
      12              : functional derivative of the energy at a given wavevector ``\bm{k}`` with respect to the
      13              : vortex positions (according to the Biot–Savart law), and applies a velocity that ensures
      14              : energy to increase (if ``α > 0`` or ``ε_{\text{target}} > 0``) at those wavevectors.
      15              : 
      16              : One should pass _either_ `α` or `ε_target` but never both. They should be positive for
      17              : energy injection (negative values lead to energy dissipation):
      18              : 
      19              : - `α` (`\alpha`) is a non-dimensional coefficient which directly sets the amplitude of the forcing velocity;
      20              : 
      21              : - `ε_target` (`\varepsilon_target`) has the units of an energy injection rate. In this case,
      22              :   the amplitude ``α`` will be adjusted over time in order to keep a roughly constant energy
      23              :   injection rate (which in general will _not_ be equal to `ε_target`, see remarks below).
      24              : 
      25              : The `modify_length` parameter can be set to `false` to avoid this forcing from increasing
      26              : (or decreasing) the vortex length. This changes the forcing velocity by removing the component that is parallel
      27              : to the local vortex curvature, such that it doesn't modify the vortex length locally.
      28              : This is experimental, and might help (or not) avoiding non-local energy transfers from large
      29              : to small scales.
      30              : 
      31              : # Extended help
      32              : 
      33              : ## Forcing definition
      34              : 
      35              : The applied forcing velocity has the basic form:
      36              : 
      37              : ```math
      38              : \bm{v}_{\text{f}} = α \bm{s}' × \tilde{\bm{v}}_{\text{s}},
      39              : ```
      40              : 
      41              : where $\tilde{\bm{v}}_{\text{s}}$ is the bandpass filtered Biot–Savart velocity between the

      43              : injected within this range of wavenumbers.
      44              : 
      45              : If ``α′ ≠ 0``, an extra term is included which does not modify the energy content at the
      46              : chosen wavenumbers:
      47              : 
      48              : ```math
      49              : \bm{v}_{\text{f}} = α \bm{s}' × \tilde{\bm{v}}_{\text{s}} - α′ \bm{s}' × \left[\bm{s}' × \tilde{\bm{v}}_{\text{s}}\right].

      52              : ## Explanations
      53              : 
      54              : This forcing attempts to increase the kinetic energy at selected wavenumbers.
      55              : Its definition starts from the expression for the kinetic energy at wavenumber ``\bm{k}``:
      56              : 
      57              : ```math
      58              : E(\bm{k}) = \frac{1}{2} |\bm{v}(\bm{k})|^2 = \frac{1}{2k^2} |\bm{ω}(\bm{k})|^2
      59              : ```
      60              : 
      61              : The idea is to translate the vortex positions by ``\bm{s}(ξ) → \bm{s}(ξ) + δ\bm{s}(ξ)`` so
      62              : that the energy ``E(\bm{k})`` increases. To determine such a displacement, one can look at
      63              : the functional derivative of ``E(\bm{k})`` with respect to the positions ``\bm{s}``:
      64              : 
      65              : ```math
      66              : \begin{align*}
      67              : \frac{δE(\bm{k})}{δ\bm{s}}
      68              : &= \frac{1}{2k^2} \bm{ω}(\bm{k}) ⋅ \frac{δ\bm{ω}^*(\bm{k})}{δ\bm{s}} + \text{c.c.},
      69              : \\
      70              : &= \frac{1}{2} \bm{ψ}(\bm{k}) ⋅ \frac{δ\bm{ω}^*(\bm{k})}{δ\bm{s}} + \text{c.c.},
      71              : \end{align*}
      72              : ```
      73              : 
      74              : where ``()^*`` denotes a complex conjugate and "c.c." means the complex conjugate of the first term.
      75              : Here we have used ``\bm{ω}(\bm{k}) = k^2 \bm{ψ}(\bm{k})``, which corresponds in physical
      76              : space to ``\bm{ω} = -∇² \bm{ψ}``.
      77              : 
      78              : The functional derivative of vorticity in Fourier space is:
      79              : 
      80              : ```math
      81              : \begin{align*}
      82              : \frac{δ\bm{ω}^*(\bm{k})}{δ\bm{s}}

      84              : \\
      85              : &= \frac{Γ}{V} \frac{δ}{δ\bm{s}} ∮ \bm{ℒ}[\bm{s}(ξ), \bm{s}(ξ)'] \, \text{d}ξ
      86              : \\
      87              : &= \frac{Γ}{V} \left[ \frac{∂\bm{ℒ}}{∂\bm{s}} - \frac{\text{d}}{\text{d}ξ} \frac{∂\bm{ℒ}}{∂\bm{s}'} \right]
      88              : \\

      91              : ```
      92              : 
      93              : where ``\bm{B}`` is a ``3×3`` complex matrix which has dimensions of an inverse length (``L^{-1}``).
      94              : 
      95              : The functional derivative of ``E(\bm{k})`` can now be written as:
      96              : 
      97              : ```math
      98              : \begin{align*}
      99              : \frac{δE(\bm{k})}{δ\bm{s}}

     101              : \\
     102              : &= \frac{Γ}{2V} [\bm{s}' × \bm{v}(\bm{k})] e^{i \bm{k} ⋅ \bm{s}} + \text{c.c.}
     103              : \\
     104              : &= ℜ \left\{ \frac{Γ}{V} [\bm{s}' × \bm{v}(\bm{k})] e^{i \bm{k} ⋅ \bm{s}} \right\}
     105              : \end{align*}
     106              : ```
     107              : 
     108              : Finally, the idea is to advect the filaments with a velocity which is parallel to this result for each forced ``\bm{k}``.
     109              : One can write this velocity as
     110              : 
     111              : ```math
     112              : \bm{v}_{\text{f}}(\bm{k}, \bm{s}) = α \, ℜ \left\{ [\bm{s}' × \bm{v}(\bm{k})] e^{i \bm{k} ⋅ \bm{s}} \right\} = α \, \bm{v}_0(\bm{k}, \bm{s})
     113              : ```
     114              : 
     115              : where ``α`` is a non-dimensional parameter setting the forcing amplitude.
     116              : 
     117              : ## Estimating the energy injection rate
     118              : 
     119              : One can also try to estimate an energy injection rate at wavevector ``\bm{k}`` associated to this velocity:
     120              : 
     121              : ```math
     122              : \frac{\text{d}E(\bm{k})}{\text{d}t}
     123              : = ∮ \frac{δE(\bm{k})}{δ\bm{s}} ⋅ \frac{\text{d}\bm{s}}{\text{d}t} \, \mathrm{d}ξ

     125              : ```
