      11              : This type of forcing does _not_ rely on an imposed normal fluid velocity. Instead, it starts from the
      12              : functional derivative of the energy at a given wavevector ``\bm{k}`` with respect to the

      17              : energy injection (negative values lead to energy dissipation):
      18              : 
      19              : - `α` (`\alpha`) is a non-dimensional coefficient which directly sets the amplitude of the forcing velocity;
      20              : 
      21              : - `ε_target` (`\varepsilon_target`) has the units of an energy injection rate. In this case,

      25              : # Extended help
      26              : 
      27              : ## Forcing definition
      28              : 
      29              : This forcing attempts to increase the kinetic energy at selected wavenumbers.
      30              : Its definition starts from the expression for the kinetic energy at wavenumber ``\bm{k}``:
      31              : 
      32              : ```math
      33              : E(\bm{k}) = \frac{1}{2} |\bm{v}(\bm{k})|^2 = \frac{1}{2k^2} |\bm{ω}(\bm{k})|^2
      34              : ```
      35              : 
      36              : The idea is to translate the vortex positions by ``\bm{s}(ξ) → \bm{s}(ξ) + δ\bm{s}(ξ)`` so
      37              : that the energy ``E(\bm{k})`` increases. To determine such a displacement, one can look at
      38              : the functional derivative of ``E(\bm{k})`` with respect to the positions ``\bm{s}``:
      39              : 
      40              : ```math
      41              : \begin{align*}
      42              : \frac{δE(\bm{k})}{δ\bm{s}}
      43              : &= \frac{1}{2k^2} \bm{ω}(\bm{k}) ⋅ \frac{δ\bm{ω}^*(\bm{k})}{δ\bm{s}} + \text{c.c.},
      44              : \\
      45              : &= \frac{1}{2} \bm{ψ}(\bm{k}) ⋅ \frac{δ\bm{ω}^*(\bm{k})}{δ\bm{s}} + \text{c.c.},
      46              : \end{align*}
      47              : ```
      48              : 
      49              : where ``()^*`` denotes a complex conjugate and "c.c." means the complex conjugate of the first term.
      50              : Here we have used ``\bm{ω}(\bm{k}) = k^2 \bm{ψ}(\bm{k})``, which corresponds in physical
      51              : space to ``\bm{ω} = -∇² \bm{ψ}``.
      52              : 
      53              : The functional derivative of vorticity in Fourier space is:
      54              : 
      55              : ```math
      56              : \begin{align*}
      57              : \frac{δ\bm{ω}^*(\bm{k})}{δ\bm{s}}
      58              : &= \frac{Γ}{V} \frac{δ}{δ\bm{s}} ∮ e^{+i \bm{k} ⋅ \bm{s}(ξ)} \bm{s}(ξ)' \, \text{d}ξ

      66              : ```
      67              : 
      68              : where ``\bm{B}`` is a ``3×3`` complex matrix which has dimensions of an inverse length (``L^{-1}``).
      69              : 
      70              : The functional derivative of ``E(\bm{k})`` can now be written as:
      71              : 
      72              : ```math
      73              : \begin{align*}
      74              : \frac{δE(\bm{k})}{δ\bm{s}}

      76              : \\

      78              : \\

      80              : \end{align*}
      81              : ```
      82              : 
      83              : Finally, the idea is to advect the filaments with a velocity which is parallel to this result for each forced ``\bm{k}``.
      84              : One can write this velocity as
      85              : 
      86              : ```math

      88              : ```
      89              : 
      90              : where ``α`` is a non-dimensional parameter setting the forcing amplitude.
      91              : 
      92              : ## Estimating the energy injection rate
      93              : 
      94              : One can also try to estimate an energy injection rate at wavevector ``\bm{k}`` associated to this velocity:
      95              : 
      96              : ```math
      97              : \frac{\text{d}E(\bm{k})}{\text{d}t}
